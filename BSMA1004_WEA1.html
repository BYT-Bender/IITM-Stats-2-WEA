Analysis

Description of a Row (Observation)

A single row in this dataset represents one individual student. It is a complete record of a student's demographic background (gender, race, and parental education), their educational support (lunch program and test preparation course status), and their numerical scores in three different subjects (Math, Reading, and Writing).

Description of Columns (Variables)

Column Name Type of Data Description in My Own Words
gender Categorical The student's self-reported gender (e.g., male or female).
race/ethnicity Categorical The student's racial or ethnic grouping (e.g., group A, B, C, D, or E).
parental level of education Categorical The highest level of education attained by the student's parent (e.g., bachelor's degree, some college, etc.).
lunch Categorical The type of school lunch the student receives, which acts as a proxy for their economic background (standard or free/reduced).
test preparation course Categorical Whether the student completed a test preparation course or not (completed or none).
math score Quantitative The student's numerical score on the math test.
reading score Quantitative The student's numerical score on the reading test.
writing score Quantitative The student's numerical score on the writing test.

Interesting Observation

I analyzed the effect of the student's lunch program and test preparation course status on their performance, using the mean score as my primary statistic.

Observation: Test Preparation Course

The analysis showed a clear benefit to students who completed the test preparation course.

Test Preparation Course Average Math Score Average Reading Score Average Writing Score
completed 69.70 73.89 74.42
none 64.08 66.53 64.50

Secondary Observation: Lunch Program

The type of school lunch a student received also had a dramatic impact on performance. Students who received the "standard" lunch (implying a more stable economic background) consistently outperformed those who received the "free/reduced" lunch.

Lunch Type Average Math Score Average Reading Score Average Writing Score
standard 70.03 71.65 70.82
free/reduced 58.92 64.65 63.02

What I Learnt/Observed:

The data reveals two powerful, yet distinct, factors influencing student performance. Firstly, a direct academic intervention like completing a test preparation course is highly effective, leading to significant score improvements, most notably an average increase of nearly 10 points in the Writing score. Secondly, socioeconomic factors, as represented by the type of school lunch, are also a profound predictor of success. Students receiving a standard lunch consistently outperformed those on a free/reduced lunch by a substantial margin, demonstrating a gap of over 11 points in the Math score. This contrast suggests that while academic support can significantly boost performance, external economic circumstances have an equally, if not more, fundamental impact on a student's baseline academic achievement.
